Pivot Agent Reference & Documentation

Full Technical Guide

LLM Intelligence

Controls how much the AI says. Normal mode is for short conversations, while Detail mode automatically provides longer explanations when you ask for more information.

  • Normal Tokens: Maximum response length for regular replies (100–200 recommended).
  • Normal Sentences: Maximum sentences in normal mode (2–3 recommended).
  • Detail Tokens: Maximum response length for detailed explanations (500–800 recommended).
  • Detail Sentences: Maximum sentences in detail mode (5–8 recommended).

Voice & Audio

Controls how the AI sounds.

  • Speech Rate: Talking speed (0.9–1.1 is most natural).
  • Volume: AI voice loudness only.
  • TTS Model: Choose between fastest English, multilingual, or standard neural voice.

STT & VAD

Controls when the AI detects you've finished speaking.

  • STT Language: Recognition language.
  • VAD Silence Timeout: Wait time after you stop speaking before sending audio to the AI (400–500 ms recommended).
  • Live Mic Energy: Displays microphone input level.

Interruptions

Controls how the AI stops when you interrupt.

  • Barge-in Cutoff Delay: Delay before stopping speech (0–50 ms recommended).
  • Audio Chunk Buffer: Small playback buffer to smooth audio (20–40 ms recommended).
  • Cutoff Mode: Instant stop or a smooth 50 ms fade-out.

Session Controls

Quick actions during a conversation.

Mute Mic: Temporarily disable microphone.
Stop/Start STT: Pause or resume speech recognition.
Interrupt Turn: Stop the AI's current reply immediately.
Reset Session: Clear conversation history.
Reconnect: Restore the connection.
Shutdown API: Stop the backend server (admin/testing use).

Detailed Parameter Deep-Dive & Latency Dynamics

1. Barge-in Cutoff Delay

What it is: The time the system waits after detecting that you've started speaking before stopping the AI's voice.

Example:
  • 0 ms: AI stops immediately when you begin talking.
  • 50 ms: AI waits 50 ms before stopping.
  • 200 ms: AI may finish its current word before stopping.

Trade-off: Lower = More responsive | Higher = More natural sounding, but you'll hear a little extra AI speech.

Recommended: 0–50 ms

2. Audio Chunk Buffer

What it is: A small delay before each TTS audio chunk is played. This acts like a mini queue to absorb network jitter.

Example:
  • 0 ms: Lowest latency, but unstable networks may cause clicks or tiny gaps.
  • 20–40 ms: Smooth playback with almost no noticeable delay.
  • 100+ ms: Playback is smoother but starts feeling delayed.

Trade-off: Lower = Faster | Higher = Smoother audio.

Recommended: 20–40 ms

3. Cutoff Mode

What it is: How the AI's voice ends when interrupted.

Instant Silence
  • Stops playback immediately.
  • No extra audio is heard.
  • Fastest response.
  • Can sound abrupt or robotic.
Soft Audio Fade (50 ms)
  • Gradually reduces the volume over about 50 ms.
  • Sounds more natural, like a human stopping mid-sentence.
  • Adds virtually no noticeable latency.
Recommended: Soft Fade for production, Instant for maximum responsiveness.

4. VAD Silence Timeout

What it is: How long the system waits after you stop speaking before deciding you've finished and sending your speech to the LLM.

Example: You say: "Tell me about... Mars."
  • 300 ms: The system may think you're finished too early and send "Tell me about...".
  • 500 ms: Waits long enough for a natural pause.
  • 1000 ms: Very safe, but the AI feels slow to respond.

Trade-off: Lower = Faster responses, but may cut you off | Higher = Better at handling pauses, but increases response latency.

Recommended: 400–500 ms
